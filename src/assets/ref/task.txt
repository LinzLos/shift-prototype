Current loop: build the core Queue Health → Queue Detail → Reassign Staff flow

Scope:
- focus only on Flow 1
- no full product expansion
- no extra navigation patterns unless required for this flow
- no new dashboards beyond what supports the flow
- no secondary flows yet
- no visual polish work unless structure is already clear

Flow goal:
An operations lead sees a queue at risk, opens that queue, reviews the cause, and reassigns a team member to stabilize it.

Core states to support:
1. Queue overview
2. Queue detail
3. Reassign staff modal or side panel
4. Confirmation / updated state

Focus:
- make the action path obvious
- show why a queue needs attention
- show enough staffing context to make a decision
- make constraints visible before confirmation
- make result of action easy to understand

Success:
- user can identify which queue needs action
- user can drill into one queue without confusion
- user can understand who is eligible for reassignment
- reassignment feels controlled, not risky or blind
- confirmation makes the system response clear

What to watch in localhost:
- is it obvious which queue is at risk and why?
- is the path from overview to action clean?
- can I understand the staffing decision without over-reading?
- are trained vs assigned states clear?
- does confirmation feel meaningful, not cosmetic?

Notes:
- strongest hiring signal is decision support under constraints
- prioritize trust, clarity, and consequences
- keep the prototype tight and believable
